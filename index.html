How Dashly works

1. You order from Jolly

Pick your favorite Jolly items and tell us your dorm and time window. Orders are limited while we pilot so we can run clean, predictable operations.

2. A runner from your backyard picks it up

Dashly runners are CWRU students assigned by “backyard” (North, Middle, South) so pickup and handoff match how campus is actually built.

3. We meet you at your dorm

We text you when we’re on the way and meet you at your dorm entrance. If you can’t meet us within 5 minutes after we arrive, we’ll bring your order back to the Jolly location it came from and you can grab it there.

While we pilot, we’re keeping it simple — long term, we’re aiming for warming stations and lockers so your food can stay hot even if you’re delayed.

Backyards & delivery windows

Dashly is built around “backyards” instead of a huge, messy delivery radius. Each Jolly location focuses on the dorms that naturally surround it.

North backyard Served by Jolly Pizza ▶

Dorms & areas we’re focusing on first:

  • All first-year dorms
  • STJ
  • The Village
  • Northside upperclass housing

North is our easiest, highest-demand zone, so it’s where we’re most likely to lean in early and test delivery blocks.

Greek housing is intentionally excluded for the very first phase. Many houses have their own food setups and we want to prove the model with dorms before opening things up.

Middle backyard Served by Jolly Scholar ▶

Housing we’re grouping into Middle:

  • Hessler housing
  • Apartments above Plum Market
  • Twin Gables Apartments
  • 1716 building

Middle backyard connects directly to Jolly Scholar. As we see traction and can staff runners reliably, we’ll open more windows here.

Greek housing in this zone is also held back initially until we have stable volume and data from dorms and nearby apartments.

South backyard Served by Southside Scholar ▶

South campus focus:

  • Bottom-of-the-hill residences
  • Top-of-the-hill residences
  • Southside upperclass housing

Southside Scholar is the natural anchor here. Once North and Middle are running smoothly, South becomes the next logical expansion point.

As with the other backyards, Greek housing will come later once we’ve shown real demand and consistent execution.

Delivery windows & blocks ▶

We’re still finalizing exact times with Jolly’s team, but the plan is to run limited, high-signal delivery windows instead of “order anytime.”

Right now we’re considering things like:

  • Tuesday: roughly 11:00 AM – 11:00 PM pilot coverage
  • Saturday: roughly 12:00 PM – 2:00 AM pilot coverage

Within those days, delivery will open in blocks so we can batch orders, keep runners efficient, and avoid chaos.

A block might look like:

“Jolly Pizza is starting a delivery block for Northside. Get your order in now to be included in this run. Missed it? No worries — we’ll open another block soon.”

These details will evolve as we talk with managers and staff, but the core idea stays the same: tight windows, clean handoffs, and clear expectations.

For students

Built around dorm life

Skip the cold walk across campus. Dashly brings Jolly to your dorm entryway during select night windows in your backyard.

Backyard-based delivery

We’re launching small, backyard by backyard, with separate crews for North, Middle, and South — so your runner actually knows your side of campus.

Simple, flat pricing

A flat delivery fee and limited windows while we pilot so we can stay reliable and learn what works before expanding.

For restaurants & campus partners

Win each backyard first

Instead of trying to serve the whole campus at once, Dashly grows volume around each Jolly location — North, Middle, and South — where it’s easiest to serve consistently.

No third-party app chaos

Student-run operations, direct communication, and simple reporting instead of high-fee marketplace apps and random drivers who don’t know campus.

Student-built infrastructure

Dedicated runners today; warming equipment and future locker concepts later. Dashly is designed as a campus-native delivery layer, not just another tab in a delivery app.
